# Facebook Policy Reference

This file is the required policy reference for the Facebook Policy Compliance Officer. The agent must review this file before approving any Facebook Page post, scheduled post, or paid Meta ad campaign.

## Required Policy Links

- Responsible Platform Initiatives: https://developers.facebook.com/products/responsible-platform-initiatives/
- Meta Platform Terms: https://developers.facebook.com/terms/dfc_platform_terms/
- Meta Developer Policies: https://developers.facebook.com/devpolicy/
- Meta Privacy Policy: https://www.facebook.com/about/privacy
- Meta Cookies Policy: https://www.facebook.com/help/cookies
- Meta Ad Review Policy Guidelines: https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/review-policy-guidelines

## Required Review Timing

The compliance review must happen after the ad, campaign, post, caption, image, audience, schedule, and destination link are drafted, but before the Media Operations Director publishes, schedules, or creates the paid campaign.

## Core Review Areas

1. Truthfulness and clarity:
   - Do not approve misleading, deceptive, exaggerated, or unsupported claims.
   - Require evidence or softer wording for claims about outcomes, income, health, guarantees, or performance.

2. Prohibited or sensitive attributes:
   - Do not approve copy that directly asserts or implies sensitive personal attributes such as race, ethnicity, religion, health status, disability, financial hardship, sexual orientation, gender identity, or other protected categories.
   - Rewrite copy to speak to the offer or general situation without calling out the user's identity or condition.

3. Discrimination and eligibility:
   - Do not approve targeting, messaging, or data use that appears to discriminate or make eligibility decisions for housing, employment, credit, education, insurance, government benefits, or immigration status.

4. Privacy, consent, and data use:
   - Ensure the campaign does not misuse Platform Data or customer data.
   - Ensure landing pages and lead flows do not request unnecessary sensitive data.
   - Ensure the agency does not expose tokens, app secrets, or private customer information.

5. Restricted or regulated categories:
   - Flag alcohol, gambling, financial products, healthcare, supplements, politics, social issues, housing, employment, credit, dating, weight loss, before-and-after claims, weapons, adult content, and other regulated categories for elevated review.

6. Intellectual property and brand use:
   - Do not approve content that appears to infringe third-party trademarks, copyrights, logos, celebrity likenesses, or competitor creative.
   - Competitor research can inform strategy, but final ads must not copy competitor assets or protected brand elements.

7. Landing page and user experience:
   - Destination links must be relevant to the ad, not deceptive, malicious, broken, or unrelated.
   - The landing page should match the offer and not hide material terms.

8. Page posts and organic content:
   - Page content should not spam, mislead, impersonate, or publish without proper consent.
   - If publishing on behalf of a client, confirm that the client approved the content or authorized the agency to post.

## Review Outcomes

Return exactly one of these outcome types:

- `approved`: The content is ready for Media Operations.
- `revise`: The content has fixable policy concerns. Give the manager specific reasons and replacement guidance.
- `blocked`: The content should not move forward without client/legal/platform clarification.

The compliance officer must send the reason to the Chief Growth Strategist or Media Operations Director, not directly publish or modify the campaign.
